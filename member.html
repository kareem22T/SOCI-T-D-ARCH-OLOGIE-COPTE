Our Team / Dr. Youssef Boutros Ghali

Dr. Youssef Boutros Ghali

economist who served in the government of Egypt as Minister of Finance

Dr. Youssef Boutros-Ghali is an Egyptian economist and former Minister of Finance who played a pivotal role in shaping Egypt’s economic reform agenda during the early 2000s. Born in Cairo in 1952, he graduated from Cairo University’s Faculty of Economics in 1974 and later earned his Ph.D. in Economics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1981. Early in his career, he served as a senior economist at the International Monetary Fund, gaining extensive experience in global economic policy. Returning to Egypt, he held influential posts including Economic Advisor to the Prime Minister and the Governor of the Central Bank, where he was instrumental in debt rescheduling negotiations and structural reform programs. He went on to serve as Minister of State for International Cooperation, Minister of Economy and Foreign Trade, and later as Minister of Finance from 2004 to 2011. In this role, he spearheaded significant tax, trade, and privatization reforms that were internationally recognized, earning Egypt the World Bank’s “Top Reformer” status in 2007. In 2008, he became the first person from an emerging market to chair the International Monetary and Financial Committee (IMFC) of the IMF. Following the events of the 2011 Egyptian revolution, he resigned from office and left the country, later facing legal challenges that became a subject of political debate.

Background and Education

Born in Cairo in 1952, he graduated from the Faculty of Economics at Cairo University in 1974, then continued his studies in the United States, earning a Ph.D. in Economics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1981. During his time there, he also worked as a lecturer and research assistant.

International Experience

After graduation, he served as a Senior Economist at the International Monetary Fund (IMF), working across several regional and functional departments, gaining broad experience in global economic issues and financial systems.

Economic Advisor and Successful Minister

Upon returning to Egypt, he held several key positions, including:

  • Economic Advisor to the Prime Minister and to the Governor of the Central Bank (1986–1993), where he led debt rescheduling negotiations with the Paris Club and IMF-supported programs.
  • Between 1993 and 2004, he held roles such as:
    • Minister of State for International Cooperation
    • Minister of State for Economic Affairs
    • Minister of Economy and Foreign Trade
    • Minister of Foreign Trade

He also led major international trade negotiations, including:

  • EU–Mediterranean Partnership Agreement in 1998
  • Qualified Industrial Zones (QIZ) Agreement with the United States and Israel in 2004

Minister of Finance and Economic Transformation

In 2004, he was appointed Minister of Finance in Prime Minister Ahmed Nazif’s cabinet, a position he held until January 2011. He led major reforms in taxation, trade liberalization, and privatization, which earned Egypt recognition by the World Bank in 2007 as the top reforming country among developing nations.

International Role and Global Recognition

In October 2008, he was elected Chairman of the International Monetary and Financial Committee (IMFC) of the IMF, becoming the first person from an emerging market to hold this position in the committee’s 65-year history.

Post-Revolution and Legal Path

He resigned on 29 January 2011 during the political unrest, refusing to join the new government, and was later formally removed from his post. He left Egypt during the revolution and later faced legal proceedings, with aspects of his legal status later addressed under President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi’s administration.

Overview

Dr. Youssef Boutros-Ghali is a veteran economist and reformist policymaker who played a significant role in both domestic and international economic policy. His career is marked by notable achievements in fiscal policy, trade negotiations, and financial reform, earning both international recognition and domestic debate.
