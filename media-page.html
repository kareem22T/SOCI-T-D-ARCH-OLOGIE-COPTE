This exceptional volume offers an in-depth journey into the world of Coptic Christianity's most treasured writings — the Gospels, biblical texts, and sacred scriptures that have been meticulously copied, decorated, and preserved by generations of scribes in monasteries and churches across Egypt.
Through detailed photography and expert commentary, the book unveils the artistry behind illuminated manuscripts, the unique Coptic script, and the theological significance of each text. Readers will discover the historical context of these works, their role in liturgical practice, and the craftsmanship of the scribes who infused each page with devotion and beauty.
The book also highlights rare examples of early translations of the Bible into the Coptic language, comparisons between different manuscript traditions, and the ways in which these sacred writings have shaped the faith, worship, and cultural heritage of Egypt's Christian community.
Perfect for historians, theologians, art lovers, and anyone interested in the preservation of spiritual heritage .
